# Site Outline

## Primary Positioning

Melissa Waller is a hands-on Principal UX Designer and experience strategist who improves the product directly, then turns recurring friction into practical quality systems, team capability, and portfolio-level decisions. Accessibility runs through the work as a built-in quality lens. It is evidence of how she builds durable systems, not the headline.

The public site should make this proof sequence legible:

1. product design and discovery
2. design-quality operating models
3. team enablement and distributed capability
4. portfolio strategy and prioritization
5. governed AI support for established workflows

This is a narrative sequence, not a required navigation order. New public work should strengthen one or more parts of it without displacing the hands-on product-design evidence that grounds the broader strategy story.

## Public Voice and Identity

`melissa-about/` owns the underlying voice and identity system. This site applies that system in concise, public-facing copy.

- Write in a clear, grounded first person. Name what Melissa designed, decided, built, or helped a team do.
- Lead with concrete verbs and outcomes: make risk visible, build a decision system, clarify a tradeoff, create a usable path, or help teams act.
- Use specific scope or evidence when it strengthens credibility; do not inflate a result or claim certainty the work does not support.
- Keep the tone practical, generous, and human. The work is rigorous without sounding managerial or abstract.
- Avoid generic transformation language, unsupported superlatives, and jargon that hides the actual action or decision.

## Public Evidence Conventions

`2026-job-search/` owns detailed case-study evidence and interview-only material. This site keeps public adaptations accurate and explicit about what each artifact represents.

- Clearly distinguish delivered historical work, public-safe reconstructions, proposed extensions, and prototypes.
- Label a reconstruction or proposed activation where a reader could otherwise mistake it for a production artifact or measured outcome.
- Describe AI work as bounded support for a workflow; people remain responsible for judgment, ownership, prioritization, and acceptance.
- Preserve sensitive operating details, private metrics, and interview-only evidence outside the public site.
- When evidence is uncertain or incomplete, describe the decision infrastructure and early signals rather than implying a long-term outcome.

## Portfolio Design System Governance

This portfolio uses the design-system adapter framework as a governance layer.

- Carbon influence: enterprise spacing discipline, action hierarchy, component structure, metadata patterns, and reusable layout primitives.
- USWDS influence: visible focus, plain language, persistent form labels, readable help text, practical validation behavior, and inclusive content structure.
- Adapter framework influence: every major UI pattern should connect to tokens, components, accessibility guidance, and reusable pattern documentation.

Core reusable patterns:

- Tokens: CSS custom properties for color, typography, spacing, radius, focus, and layout width.
- Navigation: semantic header and `nav`, descriptive links, current page state, visible keyboard focus.
- Buttons: native buttons for form actions, links for navigation, primary and secondary visual hierarchy.
- Cards and panels: repeated containers for work summaries, status callouts, and metadata.
- Forms: visible labels, contextual hints with `aria-describedby`, required fields, and CSS support for `aria-invalid`.
