One Strategy, Built in Stages

These are not separate accessibility projects. They are connected parts of one experience-quality strategy: reduce dependence on specialist intervention, build inclusive design into everyday ways of working, and give leaders a clear way to direct attention and investment as the organization grows.

My role and the organization changed during this period, but the work remained continuous. I began as the accessibility-focused designer for a 16-designer group and later led accessibility strategy for the larger Associate Experience organization that included those original teams.

What Held Across Every Stage

  • Accessibility is experience quality. Clear, usable experiences are a core product-quality expectation, not a late-stage compliance check.
  • Systems over heroics. Standards, training, decision paths, and reusable patterns make expertise available without routing every call through one specialist.
  • Shared ownership with clear escalation. Designers, product partners, and engineers own accessible outcomes. The strategy made expectations and escalation paths visible.
  • Risk directs investment. Every team needed a floor of support. Higher-risk, higher-reach, or more reusable work needed a clear path to deeper attention.

The Case Studies Are the Evidence

  • Design Quality Operating Model: established a risk-routing and learning loop for the original team.
  • Accessibility Champions and Training: moved first-pass expertise into the teams themselves.
  • AI-Assisted UX Governance: tested a tightly governed way to make review work more efficient.
  • Associate Experience Portfolio Strategy: used portfolio visibility and risk-based support to guide an organization with 70+ designers and 80+ touchpoints.

An Honest Boundary on the Final Stage

The portfolio strategy was still in progress when business circumstances changed. I present the decision infrastructure, the support model, and the early evidence it produced. I do not claim a completed long-term measurement cycle or organization-wide outcomes that I could not verify.
