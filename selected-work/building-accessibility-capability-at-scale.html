Building Accessibility Capability at Scale

Accessibility Leadership Team Enablement

Challenge

When I joined the UX organization, accessibility knowledge varied widely across the team. Designers were encountering many of the same issues during reviews, including color contrast problems, inconsistent use of components, and accessibility considerations that were being addressed too late in the design process.

As the team's primary accessibility resource, I quickly realized that answering questions one at a time would not scale. The organization needed a sustainable way to build accessibility knowledge, improve design quality, and reduce dependence on a single subject matter expert.

Approach

Rather than treating accessibility as a one-time training effort, I focused on building organizational capability.

I started by analyzing recurring accessibility defects, design review findings, and feedback from designers and stakeholders. From there, I developed a structured learning approach that focused on practical skills designers could immediately apply in their work.

The program combined:

  • Live training sessions
  • Hands-on Figma exercises
  • Real product examples
  • Job aids and checklists
  • Documentation and reference materials
  • Design review support
  • Feedback surveys and continuous improvement

The goal was not simply awareness. The goal was behavior change.

What Was Created

  • Multi-session accessibility learning program
  • Accessibility guidance and documentation hub
  • Review checklists and job aids
  • Accessibility annotation examples
  • Design review support process
  • Practical exercises based on real product work
  • Reusable resources for ongoing learning

Outcomes

  • Expanded accessibility education from an initial group of 16 designers to approximately 70 designers across teams
  • Increased accessibility awareness and confidence among designers
  • Improved consistency during design reviews and handoff
  • Reduced reliance on a single accessibility specialist
  • Embedded accessibility earlier in the design process
  • Created a foundation for ongoing accessibility capability development
