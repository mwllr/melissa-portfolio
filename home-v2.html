How I Work

A Point of View on Product Quality

Product quality should not depend on one specialist catching every issue at the end or every team relearning the same lesson.

I treat accessibility as part of how I see quality. It helps teams recognize friction, risk, and gaps in the experience early enough to do something useful about them. The practical work is making ownership clear and giving people a way to act on what they find.

A good system should make the next right decision easier.

About Melissa

I started in graphic design, where I learned that visual choices shape what people understand and trust. Over time, my work expanded into UX, design systems, accessibility, and the way teams make decisions together.

I like the parts of work most people never see: the structure behind a clear interface, the conversation that changes a decision, and the shared practice that lets a team do better work next time.
