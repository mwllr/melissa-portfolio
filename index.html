How I Work

Design the Experience

I turn complex content, needs, and constraints into clear interaction models, information architecture, and accessible behavior.

Make Quality Practical

I build standards, review paths, and decision tools that help teams catch issues earlier and avoid solving the same problem again.

Build Team Capability

I use coaching, training, shared tools, and clear escalation paths to help people become more capable, not more dependent.

Make Tradeoffs Visible

I bring product, engineering, business, and design into the same conversation when a decision affects the experience, the team, or both.

I have managed a creative team, led a web-development team, and now work as a Principal UX Designer. In each role, I have stayed close to the design work.

At Illinois, I led the College of ACES web team while personally designing every wireframe for the Program Explorer. I also co-chaired the roughly 175-person Web Implementation Guidelines Group and served on its five-person steering committee.

A Point of View on Product Quality

Product quality should not depend on one specialist catching every issue at the end or every team relearning the same lesson.

I treat accessibility as part of how I see quality. It helps teams recognize friction, risk, and gaps in the experience early enough to do something useful about them. The practical work is making ownership clear and giving people a way to act on what they find.

A good system should make the next right decision easier.

About Melissa

I started in graphic design, where I learned how thoughtful communication and visual clarity shape people's experiences.

Over time my work expanded beyond designing interfaces into leading the systems behind them. Today I help organizations strengthen design quality, improve collaboration, build team capability, and create more sustainable ways of working. Accessibility is a major strength in that work, but it isn't the whole story. Whether I'm building training, governance frameworks, or operational processes, I'm motivated by the same goal: helping people do their best work and creating better experiences for the people they serve.
